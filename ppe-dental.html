Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) in Dentistry

1. Gloves

Purpose: Prevent cross-contamination between hands, patients, and surfaces.

Types:

  • Examination Gloves: Non-sterile, single-use, available in latex, nitrile, and vinyl.
  • Surgical Gloves: Sterile, for invasive procedures, individually packaged.

Proper Use:

  • Choose correct type and size.
  • Hand hygiene before and after use.
  • Change between patients or when torn/contaminated.
  • Do not reuse disposable gloves.

2. Masks

Purpose: Protect from aerosols, splatter, and droplets; also protect patients from the clinician's respiratory emissions.

Types:

  • Surgical Masks: Levels 1â€“3 based on filtration and fluid resistance.
  • Respirators (e.g., N95): High filtration, fit-tested, used in high-risk aerosol procedures.

Proper Use:

  • Fit snugly; mold nosepiece.
  • Replace when wet, soiled, or between patients.
  • Remove using straps; avoid touching the front.

3. Eye Protection

Purpose: Prevents infection or injury from splashes and aerosols.

Types:

  • Safety Glasses: Basic protection with side shields.
  • Goggles: Snug fit for full protection.
  • Face Shields: Full-face coverage, used with a mask.
  • Patient Eyewear: Protection for patients during procedures.

Proper Use:

  • Fit properly and comfortably.
  • Clean and disinfect between patients.

4. Gowns

Purpose: Protects skin and clothing from contamination.

Types:

  • Disposable Gowns: Fluid-resistant, single use.
  • Reusable Gowns: Fabric gowns, laundered between uses.
  • Clinical Jackets: For lower-risk interactions.

Proper Use:

  • Wear when exposure to fluids is expected.
  • Ensure full coverage (neck to knees, wrists).
  • Remove carefully to avoid contamination.

Reminder: Consistent, appropriate use of PPE is key to infection control and safety in dental practice.
